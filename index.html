Welcome to StudyAstro
Made by an amateur astronomer with an interest in all types of astronomy.

I hope this site will be used to educate, entertain and teach the next generation about astronomy, so that it is not forgotten in the midst of the city lights and cities.

Want to be part of an astronomy club in Canada? Check out RASC.

This is an educational website. It's never too late to learn astronomy, even for those who have not completed their primary (High School) education. A GED can get you in the door to college level courses.

StudyAstro is a online resource of mine that is made public. Enjoy the website! Things to try:
  1. Learn about observing and join a club
  2. Learn more about the solar system
  3. Learn about everything past the solar system
  4. Take some pictures and share them with us!

How the Website is Organized:

Observation - This section includes information on coordinate systems, constellations, objects visible in the night sky, and some images of the night sky of the northern and southern hemispheres.

Solar System- As indicated, this section covers our Solar System and everything in it. It covers the Sun, planets, their moons, asteroids, comets and exotic objects like TNO's and Kuiper Belt Objects.

Extragalactic Space - This section includes information on some of the basic science used in astronomy. There is information on the various DSO (Deep Sky Objects) past the border of the Solar System and sometimes even our galaxy, the Milky Way. You will also learn about exoplanets and the latest astronomical research on similar topics.

Astrophotography- This section covers astrophotography. After you can find the constellations and several DSOs in the sky, it's a good time to get a camera and start taking pictures. There is information on what to get, how to take pictures, and what to take pictures of.

Cosmology - This section covers other galaxies and galaxies clusters. It also covers the big bang, relativity and dark matter.
